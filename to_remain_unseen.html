TO REMAIN UNSEEN

Everything moves too quickly now. Slow down to see.

Images appear for seconds before disappearing, bodies are constantly performed for screens, and visibility has become a form of labor.

We are expected to react instantly, produce endlessly, remain present all the time.

Choosing slowness becomes a way to resist.

SYSTEM/SKIN creates interruptions within this flow. Distorted images, blurred textures, and unstable bodies ask the viewer to stay longer, to look again, to exist for a moment outside the speed of consumption.

These images cannot be understood immediately, and that delay matters.

Slowness allows transformation to happen.

Human forms dissolve into creatures, identities blur, reality becomes uncertain. Nothing remains fixed long enough to become fully controlled.

In a culture obsessed with clarity and performance, opacity becomes freedom. Grain, glitches, compression, and visual decay are no longer imperfections to erase, but traces of something more intimate and alive.

To slow down is to refuse constant productivity.
To remain partially unreadable is to escape consumption.
To disappear, even briefly, from perfect visibility is its own form of rebellion.
