1) MANIFESTO - SYSTEM/SKIN


SYSTEM/SKIN is an online platform that explores sabotage not as destruction, but as a form of resistance and rebellion. Countering reproducibility and constant visibility, SYSTEM/SKIN intervenes in the digital flow, performing acts of creative sabotage that make images non-repeatable and unique. Every glitch, every distortion, every collapse of resolution becomes a mark of singularity: a refusal to conform to endless replication. Ruining an image is not a loss; it is an act of freedom. In the deliberate imperfection of pixels, in the breaking of resolution, we confront the question: is everything we see truly real? In the age of hyper-visibility, where identities are curated, filtered, and polished, glitch and decay become tools of revelation, showing what is hidden beneath the surface. SYSTEM/SKIN proposes an alternative vision of the digital world, one where images are not meant to be perfect, stable, or eternal. Here, the cracks, the errors, and the moments of distortion carry meaning. They invite reflection, slow down perception, and reveal the impermanent, fragile, and singular nature of reality. In sabotaging images, the platform reminds us that nothing digital, or human, is ever complete, flawless, or fixed. Each alteration is an act of resistance against the tyranny of replication, a celebration of the unique, the fleeting, and the unrepeatable. Through what is broken and unfinished, we glimpse the deeper truth of what it means to see, to remember, and to exist in a world that constantly asks us to appear complete and flawless.
